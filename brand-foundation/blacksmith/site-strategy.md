## 1. CUSTOMER EXCAVATION (VOC Summary)

Source: 15,191-row client database, Circle identity tiers doc, launch copy, v9 membership spec, in-chair conversation guide.

### Dream Buyer Profile
**Who:** Working men, 25-45, Gold Coast locals + professional travellers. Come in every 3-6 weeks. Already have a preferred barber. Tip well. Value consistency over price. Hate re-explaining their cut to a stranger.

### Voice of Customer — Pain Language (verbatim-grade)
- "I always ask for the same barber but sometimes he's not on"
- "Waited 40 minutes on a Saturday for a chair I can't choose"
- "Had to re-explain 'short on the sides, bit of length on top' to a stranger"
- "Walked out wondering if it'll look the same as last time"
- "You rock up, take a number, get whoever's free"
- "That's not a haircut. That's a gamble."

### What They Actually Want (desire language)
- "My barber should know my cut before I sit down"
- One barber, booked ahead, no waiting, no re-explaining, no surprises
- A place that feels like theirs — not a waiting room with plastic chairs
- To be recognised, not processed
- Consistency: same cut, same barber, same vibe, every time

### Objections to Overcome
- "I'll think about it" (→ founding scarcity + lock-in value)
- "How much?" (→ reframe: you're not paying for a cheaper cut, you're paying for a locked chair)
- "Am I using enough cuts to justify it?" (→ identity, not ROI: "Am I willing to lose my slot?")

## 3. AWARENESS LEVEL ANALYSIS

The site serves multiple visitor types at different awareness levels:

| Visitor type | Schwartz level | What they need | Page section that serves them |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Barber near me" searcher | Level 1 — Problem-unaware | Feel the vibe, see the space, want to walk in | Hero + Shop Story + Gallery + Visit |
| Existing client checking site | Level 4 — Product-aware | Book, check wait, find info | Hero CTA (Book a Cut) + sticky booking bar |
| Client curious about Circle | Level 3 — Solution-aware | Understand the membership, see the value | Circle reveal + Tier breakdown |
| Potential academy student | Level 2 — Problem-aware | See the training, understand it's real, enquire | Academy section |
| Blackrose prospect | Level 2-3 | See the salon is separate/premium | Blackrose section |

**Primary traffic:** Level 1-2 (local search, social discovery, word-of-mouth). The site must FIRST make them want to walk in, THEN open the loop on Circle.

**Key insight:** This is NOT a Circle landing page. It's the main business site that serves all arms. The Circle is the strategic reveal mid-page — the mechanism that turns a walk-in into a member. But the site must work even for someone who never clicks "Join the Circle."

## 4. HERO MECHANISM

### The Mechanism: "Your chair, not a queue number"

**What it is:** Blacksmith inverted the barbershop model. Instead of walk-in-and-hope, they built a system where your barber knows your cut, your slot is locked, and there's a room that's yours. The Circle isn't a discount program — it's the infrastructure that makes "your barber, every time" actually work.

**Why it's credible:**
- 10 years in Biggera Waters — not a pop-up
- 15,000+ clients through the chairs — this is a proven room
- 14 operators who stay (low churn = consistency for customers)
- Real physical lounge being built — tangible, not marketing language
- 47 customers already identified who spend at Empire level — the demand exists before the program launches
- SLIKR app integration — real booking infrastructure, not a paper waitlist

**Why competitors can't copy it:**
- MVN/Barber Bar sell "2 cuts + 20% off" — they sell a coupon, Blacksmith sells a chair
- Competitors don't have the lounge (capital + real estate + brand strength)
- Competitors don't have the 15K database to segment and hand-sell from
- The Circle's switching cost is identity (lose your number, your wall placement, your rate), not savings

**Hero Mechanism name:** "The Chair, Not The Queue" (internal reference for all creative)

## 5. OFFER ARCHITECTURE (Site-Level)

This is NOT a single-offer page. The site has THREE offer paths:

### Path A — Book a Cut (immediate conversion, all visitors)
- **CTA:** "Book a Cut" / "Check the Wait"
- **Destination:** SLIKR app (deep link)
- **Friction:** Zero — no signup, just book
- **Placement:** Hero (primary CTA), sticky mobile booking bar, Visit section

### Path B — Join the Circle (high-value conversion, returning/interested visitors)
- **CTA:** "Join the Circle" / "Find out your number" / "Lock in my spot"
- **Destination:** Circle signup page (separate page, not inline)
- **Friction:** Medium — needs understanding of tiers, requires commitment
- **Placement:** Circle reveal section (mid-page, after value is established), footer

### Path C — Enquire (Academy + Blackrose)
- **CTA:** "Enquire" / "Book Blackrose"
- **Destination:** Contact form / SLIKR
- **Friction:** Low
- **Placement:** Respective service sections

**CTA hierarchy on every viewport:**
1. "Book a Cut" (always visible — hero + sticky bar on mobile)
2. "Join the Circle" (appears after the Circle reveal section)
3. Service-specific CTAs (in-section only)

## 6. SELLING SEQUENCE — Section Funnel Structure

This is the page architecture mapped to the direct-response selling sequence. NOT a flat brochure. Every section has a job.

### Section 1 — RAPPORT / HOOK (Hero)
**Job:** Stop the scroll. Establish identity. Make them feel the room before they've walked in.

- Full-bleed shop photo (dark, moody interior — TLB_4429.jpg or similar)
- Scotland wordmark (logo)
- Headline: identity-level ("More than a barbershop." or similar — NOT "Welcome to Blacksmith")
- Sub: one line that earns the click ("A decade of sharp cuts, good company, and proper craft.")
- Eyebrow: "Est. 2015 — Biggera Waters, Gold Coast"
- **CTA pair:** "Book a Cut" (gold, primary) + "Check the Wait" (ghost)
- Vibe: you've just walked past this place and the door is open. The energy pulls you in.

**Awareness served:** Level 1-2 (make them curious) + Level 4 (give them the book button immediately)

---

### Section 2 — OPEN LOOP / STORY (The Shop)
**Job:** Build rapport. Earn trust through specificity and proof, not claims. Open the loop on why this place is different.

- Eyebrow: "The Shop"
- Headline: "Ten years in the chair. The Gold Coast keeps coming back."
- Body: Short paragraph — specifics that prove the room (state-of-the-art chairs, cold drink in hand, barbers who know your name, one minute from Harbour Town, easy parking)
- **Implicit loop opened:** "Why do people keep coming back?" → answered by the Circle reveal later
- No CTA in this section — it's a bridge, not a conversion point

**Awareness served:** Level 1-2 (story builds trust before any ask)

---

### Section 3 — STACK VALUE (Services Triptych)
**Job:** Show the three arms. Prove breadth. Give every visitor type a door to walk through.

- Three visual cards, full-bleed photos:
  1. **Barbering** ("The Chair") — book a cut
  2. **Blackrose Salon** ("Precision, colour & transformation") — book Blackrose
  3. **Academy** ("Learn the trade") — enquire
- Each card: photo + eyebrow + headline + link
- Grid layout, hover reveals

**Awareness served:** Level 2-3 (solution-aware visitors find their arm)

---

### Section 4 — PROOF (Blackrose + Academy Detail)
**Job:** Expand on the two secondary arms. Build credibility for each. Serve visitors who came specifically for salon or training.

- **Blackrose block:** two-column (photo + copy). "Precision, colour & transformation." Soft lighting, refined timber, calmer pace. CTA: "Book Blackrose"
- **Academy block:** two-column reversed. "Learn it from people who live it." Real chairs, real clients, real certification. CTA: "Enquire"

**Awareness served:** Level 2-3 (specific interest in salon/academy)

---

### Section 5 — WOW / SOCIAL PROOF (Gallery)
**Job:** Let the room do the talking. Visual proof that the space is premium without saying "premium."

- Headline: "The room does half the talking."
- Photo grid (4-column desktop, 2-column mobile) — interior shots, close-ups, vibe
- No copy needed. The photos ARE the proof.
- Subtle desaturation filter, hover reveals full colour

**Awareness served:** Level 1-2 (visual proof for undecided visitors)

---

### Section 6 — THE MECHANISM REVEAL (Circle Introduction)
**Job:** This is the selling turn. Open with the problem (walk-in model), reveal the mechanism (Circle), create desire. This section converts browsers into Circle-curious.

- Eyebrow: "Blacksmith Circle"
- Headline: "Stop being next in line." OR "Your chair. Your barber. Every time."
- Problem paragraph: "Walk-ins were built for the barbershop. Not for you." (2-3 sentences max — the re-explain, the wait, the stranger)
- Mechanism reveal: "Blacksmith Circle is the end of that. One barber. Your barber. Booked ahead. No waiting. No re-explaining. No surprises."
- **Comparison table** (stripped down):

  | | Walk-in | Circle Member |
  |---|---|---|
  | Your barber | Whoever's free | Always yours |
  | Wait time | 20-60 min | Zero |
  | Explain your cut | Every time | Once |
  | The lounge | Doesn't exist | Yours |

- **Tier cards (The Cut / The Club / The Empire)** — identity-first, not price-first:
  - The Cut $59/mo — "Your chair, locked in."
  - The Club $129/mo — "Tuesday afternoon is yours."
  - The Empire $229/mo — "Your name on the wall."
- CTA: "Join the Circle" / "Find out your number"
- Scarcity line: "Founding member spots are limited. Once they're gone, the price goes up."

**Awareness served:** Level 3-4 (solution/product-aware, ready to consider)

---

### Section 7 — VISIT / LOGISTICS (The Practical Close)
**Job:** Remove final friction. Give them everything they need to walk in or book.

- Three-column grid:
  1. **Find Us** — address, "One minute from Harbour Town", parking note
  2. **Hours** — operating hours
  3. **Book** — "Walk in or lock in your chair on the app" + SLIKR link
- Background: panel colour (#121214) with border lines

**Awareness served:** All levels (practical info closes the loop)

---

### Section 8 — FOOTER (Brand Reinforcement)
**Job:** Reinforce the brand. Last impression = the wordmark and the vibe.

- Scotland wordmark (logo, larger)
- "Est. 2015 — Biggera Waters"
- Minimal links
- Dark, clean, final

---

### PERSISTENT ELEMENT — Mobile Booking Bar
**Job:** Never lose the booking conversion on mobile.

- Fixed bottom bar, appears on scroll (mobile only, <820px)
- Single CTA: "Book a Cut" (gold button, full-width)
- Always accessible — the site never loses the immediate-action path

---

## 7. WHAT THIS IS NOT

- **Not a flat brochure.** Every section has a strategic job in the selling sequence.
- **Not a Circle landing page.** The Circle is ONE section (Section 6) within a site that serves all arms. A dedicated Circle landing page can be built separately later.
- **Not a discount page.** No prices appear until the Circle tier cards. No "save $X" language anywhere.
- **Not generic.** Every headline, proof point, and CTA is grounded in real VOC and the 15K client database.
