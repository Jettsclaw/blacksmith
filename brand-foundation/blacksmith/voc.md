# BLACKSMITH BARBERS — Voice of Customer (VOC)

**Source:** Google reviews (202, 4.7 stars), Facebook reviews (258, 94% recommend), App Store (4.5 stars), brain/02-projects/blacksmith-brand.md, Circle callsheet data (62 qualifying regulars)
**Last updated:** 2026-06-02

---

## Awareness Level: Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware

Most Blacksmith customers already know they want a good barber (solution-aware). The pain is latent — they don't articulate "I need a membership" but they DO articulate the frustrations that a membership solves: inconsistency, waiting, re-explaining. The Circle pitch moves them from solution-aware ("I need a reliable barber") to product-aware ("Blacksmith Circle locks my chair").

---

## Top Pains (real language)

1. **Inconsistency / barber roulette**
   - "You rock up, take a number, get whoever's free"
   - "Had to re-explain 'short on the sides, bit of length on top' to a stranger"
   - "Walked out wondering if it'll look the same as last time"
   - → The fear: a haircut is a gamble when you can't choose your barber

2. **Wait times**
   - Business messaging repeatedly addresses this ("skip the stress", "zero wait time", "reduce your wait time")
   - App was built specifically to solve this — signals it was the #1 historical complaint
   - Saturday walk-ins historically 30-40+ minute waits

3. **No recognition / feeling like a number**
   - "I always ask for the same barber but sometimes he's not on"
   - The walk-in model treats every visit as a first visit
   - → Desire: "My barber should know my cut before I sit down"

4. **Can't lock in preferred barber**
   - Customers name specific barbers in reviews (Locky B, Zayn, Lachlan, Korey, Justin, Cam)
   - Strong personal attachment — but no guarantee of availability
   - → The latent pain Circle solves

## Customer Types (5 segments from reviews + data)

### 1. The Loyal Regular
- **Who:** Male, 25-45, been coming 1-2+ years, has "their" barber
- **Visits:** Every 3-4 weeks, 12-17/year
- **Spend:** $50-70/visit, $600-1,200/year
- **Pain:** Occasional scheduling miss, wait on busy days
- **Desire:** Lock in their barber permanently
- **Circle fit:** The Cut or The Club
- **Language:** "Always gets me right", "my barber"

### 2. The Barber-Hopper Who Stopped
- **Who:** Male, 20s-30s, tried 3+ GC barbershops before finding Blacksmith
- **Visits:** Irregular at first, then settled into routine
- **Pain:** Inconsistency everywhere else
- **Desire:** Never have to look again
- **Circle fit:** The Cut (starter lock-in)
- **Language:** "I've tried other barbers... I keep coming back"

### 3. The VIP / High-Frequency
- **Who:** 973 identified (20+ visits OR $1,500+ LTV), top 47 are Empire-prime
- **Visits:** 20-30+/year, some weekly
- **Spend:** $1,500-4,000+/year
- **Pain:** Deserves more than the same experience as a walk-in
- **Desire:** Recognition, exclusivity, status
- **Circle fit:** The Empire (founding wall, merch, events)
- **Language:** "This is my shop"

### 4. The Dad / Family Bringer
- **Who:** Fathers bringing sons/grandsons, multi-generational
- **Visits:** 2-3 people per visit, monthly
- **Pain:** Finding a place the whole family is comfortable
- **Desire:** A family barbershop that knows his kids' names too
- **Circle fit:** The Club (2 slots/month covers dad + son)
- **Language:** "Will not go anywhere else" (said about grandson)

### 5. The Young Bloke / Student
- **Who:** 16-24, high school or uni, wants sharp fades
- **Visits:** Every 2-3 weeks (high maintenance styles)
- **Pain:** Price-sensitive but wants quality
- **Desire:** Look sharp, feel confident, the social experience
- **Circle fit:** Aspirational — not yet, but future pipeline
- **Language:** "Does a good cut", names specific barbers

## Key Strategic Insight

The dominant customer narrative is: **"I tried everywhere else, then I found Blacksmith and never left."**

This is the perfect foundation for Circle — customers already behave like members (loyal, named-barber, regular cadence). Circle just formalises what they're already doing and adds the status + guarantee layer they can't get from a walk-in model.

The emotional core is **mateship** — "these blokes become your mates."
The functional proof point is **precision fades** — "always gets me right."
The mechanism is **your chair, not a queue number.**
