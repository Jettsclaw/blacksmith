# BLACKSMITH BARBERS — Competitor Spy

**Source:** Web research, competitor websites, review platforms
**Last updated:** 2026-06-02

---

## Competitive Landscape Summary

No Gold Coast competitor barbershop has a membership/loyalty model, strong brand identity beyond "we cut hair well", or community-building beyond the next appointment. Blacksmith owns the open lane: membership-driven, identity-rich, relationship-first barbershop.

---

## Competitor 1: Blends Barbershop (Burleigh Heads)

**Location:** 5/1837 Gold Coast Highway, Burleigh Heads
**Website:** blends-barbershop.com
**Ratings:** 4.9 stars, 1,400+ reviews on Fresha

**Positioning:** Modern grooming destination. Precision + experience. Beer on tap, named barbers with tiered skill levels (Junior, Specialist, Master). Expanded into aesthetics — facials, teeth whitening, scalp treatments.

**Pricing:**
- Standard Haircut: $45
- Razor/Zero Fade: $50
- Master Barber Fade: $90 (1hr)
- Presidential Package: $105
- Premium Facial: $110

**Strengths:**
- Strong review volume and rating
- Expanded service menu increases ticket size
- Clean site, social proof-heavy

**Weaknesses:**
- Generic messaging — "no shortcuts" could be any barbershop. No real brand story.
- Booking outsourced to Fresha (friction)
- FAQ copy-pasted from a Toronto location ("Sota Studio") — sloppy
- Aesthetics bolted on, not integrated
- No membership/loyalty model — purely transactional
- No community or retention play

**Counter-position:** Blends is the "modern grooming menu" play — wide services, premium pricing, but no soul. Blacksmith owns identity, community, and belonging where Blends is just a transaction with good reviews.

## Competitor 2: Barber Industries (Pacific Fair, Broadbeach)

**Location:** Shop 1575, Pacific Fair Shopping Centre, Broadbeach
**Website:** barberindustries.com.au/gold-coast
**Social:** ~1,293 Instagram followers (small for a franchise)

**Positioning:** Heritage/nostalgia franchise — "blending tradition, aesthetics and nostalgia of the old world barbershop with modern comforts." Multi-location (not GC-only). Targets "the modern man" broadly.

**Pricing:** Not published. Shopping centre location suggests mid-range ($40-60).

**Strengths:**
- High-traffic location (Pacific Fair)
- Franchise brand recognition
- Gift vouchers, product retail

**Weaknesses:**
- Franchise = no local identity. One of many locations, not "your" shop.
- No online booking in 2026
- No pricing transparency
- Website bloated with blog content, weak conversion
- Tiny social following for a franchise
- Shopping centre location = convenient but soulless

**Counter-position:** Barber Industries is the "corporate franchise in a mall" play. No roots, no community, no personality. Blacksmith is independent, owner-operated — you know the barber's name and they know yours. The anti-franchise.

## Competitor 3: Connolly & Co Barbers (Robina)

**Location:** East Quay Shopping Centre, Suite 6, 217-219 Ron Penhaligon Way, Robina
**Website:** connollyandco.com.au
**Ratings:** 5 stars (volume unclear)

**Positioning:** "Where Every Cut Tells a Story." Traditional barber ritual — complimentary whiskey, genuine client relationships. "Leave the week at the door. This is your time." Claims 20 years + 50 years combined experience.

**Pricing:** Not published anywhere.

**Strengths:**
- Closest in feel to Blacksmith's positioning — relationship-driven, ritual-focused
- Experience/tenure as trust signal (20 years)
- Clean website design

**Weaknesses:**
- Content-thin — almost no detail on services, team, or pricing
- Shopping centre location undermines "authentic traditional" positioning
- Weak social presence (Facebook-primary, dated for GC demo)
- "Where Every Cut Tells a Story" is vague — what story? Whose?
- No loyalty/retention mechanism
- Limited hours

**Counter-position:** Connolly talks the "your barbershop" game but can't deliver it — hidden in a shopping centre, no pricing clarity, thin digital presence, no retention model. Blacksmith takes the same positioning and actually delivers with membership, real community, and a standalone location that matches the brand.
