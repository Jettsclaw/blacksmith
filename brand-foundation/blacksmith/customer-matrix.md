## 5 Customer Types x Angles

### 1. The Loyal Regular

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Who** | Male, 25-45, GC local, been coming 1-2+ years. Has "their" barber. |
| **Visits** | Every 3-4 weeks (12-17/yr) |
| **Spend** | $50-70/visit, $600-1,200/yr |
| **Pain** | Occasional scheduling miss, wait on busy Saturdays, no guarantee his barber is on |
| **Desire** | Lock in his barber permanently. Never re-explain his cut. |
| **Awareness** | Solution-aware → Product-aware (knows Blacksmith, needs to see Circle value) |
| **Circle tier** | The Cut ($59/mo) or The Club ($129/mo) |
| **Hook angle** | "You already come here every month. Circle just makes sure your barber's always there." |
| **Objection** | "I already come here anyway" → "You come here AND wait. AND hope he's on. Circle fixes both." |
| **CTA** | "Lock in your chair" |

### 2. The Barber-Hopper Who Stopped

| Dimension | Detail |

| **Pain** | Inconsistency everywhere else. "Had to re-explain every time." |
| **Desire** | Never have to look for a barber again |
| **Awareness** | Problem-aware → Solution-aware (knows the pain, just found the fix) |
| **Circle tier** | The Cut ($59/mo — entry-level lock-in) |
| **Hook angle** | "You've tried every barber on the Coast. You stopped here. Now lock it in." |
| **Objection** | "I just got here, not ready for membership" → "Founding spots are limited. The regulars who've been here 3 years are locking in now." |
| **CTA** | "Stop looking. Start locking." |

### 4. The Dad / Family Bringer

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Who** | Father 30-50, brings 1-2 sons. Multi-generational loyalty. |
| **Visits** | Monthly (2-3 cuts per visit) |
| **Spend** | $100-180/visit (dad + kids), $1,200-2,000+/yr |
| **Pain** | Finding one place the whole family is comfortable. Coordinating multiple bookings. |
| **Desire** | A family barbershop that knows his kids' names too |
| **Awareness** | Solution-aware (already chose Blacksmith for the family) |
| **Circle tier** | The Club ($129/mo — 2 locked slots covers dad + son) |
| **Hook angle** | "Your son's barber should know his name before he sits down. Not after." |
| **Objection** | "My kids don't come every month" → "The Club gives you 2 locked slots. Use one for you, gift the other when your boy needs a trim." |
| **CTA** | "Lock in the family chair" |

### 5. The Young Bloke / Student

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Who** | Male, 16-24, high school or uni. Wants sharp fades, high-maintenance styles. |
| **Visits** | Every 2-3 weeks (17-26/yr — highest frequency) |
| **Spend** | $35-45/visit (student pricing), $600-1,100/yr |
| **Pain** | Price-sensitive but wants quality. Embarrassed by a bad cut. |
| **Desire** | Look sharp, feel confident, the social experience with mates |
| **Awareness** | Problem-aware (knows bad cuts exist, wants to avoid them) |
| **Circle tier** | Aspirational — not the Phase 0 target, but future pipeline |
| **Hook angle** | "Your mates notice the fade before they notice the fit. Get it right every time." |
| **Objection** | "Can't afford a membership" → Not the target for Circle launch. Convert via standard booking, nurture into The Cut when they're earning. |
| **CTA** | "Book your barber" (standard, not Circle) |
