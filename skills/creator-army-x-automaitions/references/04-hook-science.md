# Hook Science

The hook is the most important 3 seconds of any piece of content. It
determines whether the rest of your work gets seen or dies in the feed.

## The Feel / Look / Think Framework

Every effective hook triggers at least one of these. The best hooks
trigger all three simultaneously.

### FEEL Hooks (Emotional Triggers)
| Emotion | Example | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Fear | "Your moisturiser is making your acne worse" | Threat to current behavior |
| FOMO | "Almost sold out — here's why" | Social proof + scarcity |
| Humor | Boyfriend stealing skincare scenario | Relatability + entertainment |
| Anger | "They've been lying to you about [X]" | Injustice triggers engagement |
| Nostalgia | "Remember when [shared memory]..." | Emotional connection |
| Shock | Unexpected reveal, bold claim | Pattern interrupt |
| Curiosity | "I took mushrooms for 30 days" | Open loop |

### LOOK Hooks (Visual Pattern Interrupts)
- Fast movement in first frame (hand grab, product toss, quick pan)
- ASMR / satisfying textures (works especially well in feed)
- High contrast colour (bright against muted background)
- Unexpected object in frame (something that doesn't belong)
- Face close-up with strong emotion (genuine, not acted)
- Split screen / side-by-side (instant comparison)
- Text-first frame (bold statement, works with sound off)

### THINK Hooks (Cognitive Triggers)
- **Bold claim:** "This $8 product outperforms $200 serums"
- **Social proof:** "47,000 five-star reviews can't be wrong"
- **Curiosity gap:** "The one thing dermatologists won't tell you"
- **Relatability:** "Does your boyfriend always use your skincare?"
- **Question:** "Are you sick of products that promise and never deliver?"
- **Challenge:** "I tested every protein powder. Here's my honest ranking."
- **Controversy:** "Unpopular opinion: [common practice] is ruining your [X]"

## Hook Formulas (Copy-and-Adapt)

### Formula 1: "I [did unusual thing] for [time period]"
> "I took mushrooms for 30 days and here's what happened"
> "I used only drugstore skincare for a month — here's my honest take"
> "I replaced all my tools with this one brand for 6 months"

### Formula 2: "Number one worst [category] for your [outcome]"
> "Number one worst fruit for your waistline"
> "The worst exercise for back pain (everyone does it)"
> "The number one ingredient destroying your gut health"

### Formula 3: "[Common thing] is like [unexpected metaphor]"
> "Skincare for acne is like painting a dead leaf green"
> "Running ads without creative is like fishing without bait"
> "Most protein powders are basically expensive sugar water"

### Formula 4: "Does your [person] always [relatable behavior]?"
> "Does your boyfriend always steal your skincare?"
> "Does your dog do this when you open the fridge?"
> "Does your trainer always recommend this? They shouldn't."

### Formula 5: "I finally [bought/tried] [trending thing] and..."
> "I finally bought one of those viral [products] and here's my review"
> "I finally tried the [trend] everyone's talking about"

### Formula 6: "[Specific number] [authority] recommends this"
> "9 out of 10 dermatologists recommend this one ingredient"
> "The one brand Navy SEALs actually use"

### Formula 7: "Stop [common mistake] — do this instead"
> "Stop putting ice on injuries — do this instead"
> "Stop scaling your ads before you do this one thing"

## Sound-Off Design

50%+ of feed viewers watch with sound off. Every hook must work visually:
- First frame should have text overlay with the hook statement
- Bold sans-serif font, high contrast (white on dark or dark on light)
- Maximum 8 words on screen at once
- The text IS the hook — not a subtitle of what's being said

## Hook Testing Protocol

1. Film 5+ hooks for every piece of content
2. Same body, different opening 3 seconds
3. Test in feed with equal budget for 48 hours
4. Judge by Hook Rate (3-second view / impression)
5. Kill bottom performers, iterate on top 2
6. Winning hooks get reused across different bodies
