# Lo-Fi Production Guide

The best ads don't look like ads. They look like organic content that
happened to be about a product. Everything in this guide optimizes for
phone-first, natural, authentic production. No studio. No crew. No
excuse not to shoot today.

## The Golden Rule

If it looks like it was made by a marketing department, it will
perform like it was made by a marketing department. Viewers have
developed an immune system against polished content. Lo-fi IS the
production standard for high-performing social ads.

## Phone Setup

### Camera Settings
- Shoot in 4K if your phone supports it (gives room to crop/reframe)
- Lock exposure and focus (tap and hold on your subject)
- Shoot in 4:5 or 9:16 depending on placement
- Turn OFF beauty mode, filters, and HDR (looks artificial)
- Clean your lens (sounds dumb, makes a real difference)

### Audio
- Phone mic is fine for 80% of content
- For voiceover: AirPods or any wireless earbuds as a mic
- Record in a quiet room, avoid echo (small room with soft surfaces)
- If outdoors: use a simple lav mic ($15 clip-on) or face away from wind

## Lighting (Keep It Natural)

Natural light is better than any ring light. Period.

### The Window Rule
- Face toward a window. That's it. That's the lighting setup.
- Overcast days = the best softbox nature provides
- Avoid direct sunlight (harsh shadows, squinting)
- Golden hour (sunrise/sunset) for warm, flattering content

### If You Must Use Artificial Light
- One soft light source, slightly above and to the side
- Never light from directly in front (flat and unflattering)
- Never use the flash
- $30 ring light is fine for product close-ups

## Movement and Framing

### Camera Movement
- Static shots are boring. Move the camera.
- Walk toward/away from the product
- Slow pan across a surface or setup
- Hand-held shake is GOOD (feels organic, not produced)
- Quick cuts between angles keep attention

### Framing Rules
- Fill the frame. Don't leave dead space.
- Eye-level or slightly above for talking head
- Get CLOSE for product shots (detail sells)
- Avoid centering everything (off-center feels more natural)
- Multiple angles of the same moment (shoot it 3 times, different positions)

### B-Roll Essentials
For any product, film these 5 shots:
1. Product in hand / being used (close-up)
2. Product in context / lifestyle setting (wide)
3. Texture / detail shot (macro, slow motion if possible)
4. Reaction shot (face showing genuine response)
5. The "money shot" (the visual that makes you want to buy)

## Working with Talent

### Finding Creators
- Your customers ARE your talent. Repost and amplify.
- Friends, staff, family — authentic faces beat models
- Brief them loosely. Over-scripted = over-produced.
- Give them bullet points, not a script. Let them use their own words.

### Directing Non-Actors
- "Tell me about the first time you tried this" (story prompt)
- "What would you say to someone who's thinking about buying this?"
- "Show me how you use it in the morning" (action prompt)
- Film the conversation, not a performance
- Keep rolling between takes — the best moments happen when they relax

### The 80/20 of Talent
- 80% of your content should feature real people (customers, founders, team)
- 20% can be product-only or lifestyle B-roll
- Diverse talent = diverse audience reach (Meta serves each face to its audience)

## Editing Principles (Not Software Tutorials)

### Pacing
- First 3 seconds: FAST. Quick cuts, text, movement.
- Body: Slow down slightly. Let points breathe. But never stagnate.
- CTA: Speed up again. Urgency in the rhythm.
- If it feels slow to you, it's VERY slow to the viewer.

### Captions Are Mandatory
- Burned-in captions (not platform auto-generated)
- Bold, readable, high contrast
- Maximum 2 lines on screen at once
- Highlight key words in a different colour
- 50%+ of viewers watch with sound off. Captions ARE the content.

### Text Overlays
- Use for key stats, claims, and the hook
- Bold sans-serif, centered or lower-third
- Don't put text where the platform UI sits (top and bottom edges)
- Animate text in (simple fade or pop, nothing flashy)

### Music and Sound
- Trending sounds boost organic reach
- For ads: use royalty-free or licensed music
- Music should enhance mood, not overpower voice
- Sound effects on cuts/transitions add punch (subtle whoosh, click)

### Length Guidelines
- Hook test clips: 5-15 seconds
- Standard ad: 15-45 seconds (sweet spot for Meta)
- Long-form ad/sVSL: 60-180 seconds
- Organic content: varies, but keep the energy up regardless of length

### The Edit Quality Check
Before publishing, ask:
1. Would I watch this if it wasn't my brand?
2. Does it look like organic content or a corporate ad?
3. Can I understand it with sound off?
4. Is the first frame strong enough to stop a scroll?
5. Can I cut 20% without losing the message? (If yes, cut it.)
