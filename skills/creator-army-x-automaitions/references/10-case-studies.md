# Case Studies

Real examples with real numbers from Simon Beard's portfolio and Creator Army clients.

## Case Study 1: Culture Kings — The $600M Content Flywheel

### The Origin
Started 2008. Market stall at Carrara Markets, Gold Coast. Simon and Tahnee
Beard. Streetwear. No investors. No debt. Grew to $600M valuation and NYSE
listing. 100% bootstrapped.

### The Marketing Edge
Culture Kings was early to every platform shift that mattered:
- **Facebook Ads (2010):** One of the first Australian retailers to go heavy on Meta
- **Shopify (2015):** Early adopter when "online won't work for streetwear" was consensus
- **Afterpay (2016):** Second business to ever use it
- **TikTok (2018):** First major Australian retailer on the platform
- **AI Creative (2024+):** Now applying AI to the creative production system

### The Content Machine
At peak, Culture Kings was producing:
- 50-70% of ads were being refreshed in morning standups
- Creative testing was the daily operating rhythm
- The content volume was the moat — competitors couldn't match the pace

### The Key Lesson
"The advantage went to whoever moved first and actually implemented. Not
whoever read about it. Not whoever bought the software. Whoever actually shipped."

## Case Study 2: Comfrt — The Future of Retail

### What Simon Saw
Comfrt represented the future of retail to Simon. The days of racing to have
the biggest limitless aisle with as much stock as possible are gone. Instead,
the model is:
- Curated product selection
- Content-driven discovery (not search-driven)
- Community as the distribution channel
- The product IS the content

### The Creative = Targeting Insight
Comfrt proved that a single product could find multiple audiences through
creative diversity alone:
- Same towel product → "luxury bathroom upgrade" creative → found interior design audience
- Same towel product → "gym essential" creative → found fitness audience
- Same towel product → "perfect gift" creative → found gifting audience
- NO targeting changes. Only creative changes. Meta found each audience.

### The Content Velocity Lesson
"50 to 70% sometimes of our ads, do the morning standup, go through them,
refresh. That's the rhythm. Creative is never done."

## Case Study 3: The Three Variables in Action

### Strike Anywhere (Simon's DTC Brand)
- Running ~$64K/week in Meta spend
- ~$23 CPA, ~2,800 purchases/week
- USD revenue, USD cost caps

### The Offer Test That Changed Everything
- **Before:** CPA $27, AOV $70
- **Changed offer:** Bundled to get AOV to $120
- **Result:** CPA rose by $5-6 (to ~$33) BUT GP contribution went up $20-30 per transaction
- **Lesson:** A slightly higher CPA with a much higher AOV = dramatically more profit
- "Absolute game changer" — most people never test the offer, they only test creative

### The Budget Truth
You cannot scale budget without creative fuel. Adding budget to a stale ad set
is like turning up the volume on a bad song. The rhythm is:
1. Create winning creative → 2. Scale budget behind it → 3. Create more
creative before the winners fatigue → 4. Repeat

## Case Study 4: Hudson/Comfort — Content Volume as Moat

### The Numbers
- 5,000 pieces of content per week
- 13,000 live ads running simultaneously
- This volume IS the competitive moat

### Why Volume Wins
Meta's AI needs fuel. The more diverse creative you feed it, the more audiences
it can find, the more efficiently it spends your budget. Brands running 5 ads
are competing against brands running 13,000. The algorithm rewards volume.

### What This Means for Smaller Brands
You don't need 13,000 ads. But you do need:
- Minimum 8-20 active creatives at all times
- 3-5 new creatives added every week
- Genuine diversity across the 12 creative types
- A production system that doesn't require a film crew for every shoot

This is why lo-fi, phone-first production matters. You can't produce volume
at Hollywood quality. You CAN produce volume at organic-content quality.
And organic-content quality converts better anyway.

## Case Study 5: PureU — Creative as Targeting

### The Barrier/Motivator Excavation
PureU is a natural acne supplement. The customer excavation revealed:
- **Barrier 1:** "I've tried topical treatments and they don't work"
- **Barrier 2:** "Supplements for skin sounds too good to be true"
- **Barrier 3:** "I don't understand how a pill fixes my face"

### The Metaphor That Unlocked It
"Using skincare to fix acne is like painting a dead leaf green. You're not
fixing the root cause."

This single metaphor:
1. Named the failed solution (topical skincare)
2. Explained the unique mechanism (internal, not external)
3. Made the complex instantly tangible
4. Worked as a hook, a body element, AND a CTA driver
5. Could be filmed 5 different ways for 5 different ad variants

### The Lesson
One great insight, expressed clearly, is worth more than a hundred generic
ads. The customer excavation process finds the insight. The selling sequence
delivers it. The production system scales it.
