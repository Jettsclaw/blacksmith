# Landing Page Copy

The landing page is the bridge between the ad and the sale. It carries everything CA does — the Godfather Offer from `14-offer-architecture.md`, the unique mechanism from `13-hero-mechanism.md`, the customer language from `/voc` — and renders it as the deliverable that closes the loop.

This file covers three things:

1. **Two landing page types** — opt-in vs. sales page. Pick wrong and conversion collapses.
2. **The 17-Step Secret Selling System** — Sabri Suby's full sales-letter structure for sales pages. Used by King Kong to generate $400M+ across 167 niches.
3. **The Magic Lantern Technique** — the nurture sequence that runs between opt-in and sale, turning the 97% of "not buying right now" prospects into eventual customers.

Plus how to brief `automaitions` (the HTML builder skill) so the landing page that comes out matches the strategic intent rather than being a generic template.

---

## Pre-flight: the 6 decisions + the pushback protocol

Before a single section of landing-page copy gets written, force the operator through the **6 decisions** in `17-automaitions-handoff.md` § Pre-brief discipline. They produce the unmovable spine of the page — feeling, audience+anti-audience, hero object, job verb, sectional cuts, three-second memory.

When an answer hedges — *"premium and trustworthy"*, *"feels modern"*, *"clean and professional"* — that's a failure state, not an answer. The pushback:

> *"That's a failure state. Those adjectives describe every brand in your category, so they produce every landing page in your category. The answer has to be a specific image, a specific phrase, a specific feeling that wouldn't fit a competitor. One sentence. Pick one."*

Don't accept the second hedge either. Iterate until each of the 6 answers is concrete enough that a reader could repeat it back the next day. If you skip this step, you'll write copy that scores "fine" on a draft review and "generic" on the eye-tracking heatmap.

Cross-references for the banned-adjective list and the rest of the discipline: `22-polish-pass.md` § Audit 7 Copy.

## Two landing page types — pick the right one

The biggest mistake operators make is using a sales page when they need an opt-in page (or vice versa). The two have completely different jobs and completely different structures.

| Type | Job | Length | What it asks for | Conversion benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Opt-in page** | Deliver an HVCO (lead magnet), capture email | Short (1 screen) | Name + email | 20-40% for cold traffic |
| **Sales page** | Present the Godfather Offer, close the sale | Long (long-scroll) | Credit card | 1-5% for cold, 10-30% for warm |

### When to use an opt-in page

Use an opt-in page when:
- The product is high-consideration (>$300) — cold traffic almost never buys high-ticket on first visit
- The sales conversation needs to happen on a phone call (B2B services, coaching, consulting)
- You want to build an email list as the primary CAC investment
- Your unit economics need 5-30 day nurture to convert (long sales cycle)

The opt-in page is **the first transaction**. The currency is the email, not money — but it's still a transaction. Treat it that way. The HVCO needs to be valuable enough that the prospect thinks *"if they're giving this away for free, imagine what their paid product is like."*

### When to use a sales page

Use a sales page when:
- The product is low-to-mid ticket ($20-$300) and cold-buy viable
- The category is impulse-friendly (DTC consumables, fashion, beauty, gifting)
- You have strong proof / social proof that closes objections in-page
- The unit economics work on a first-purchase basis (no nurture required)

Most DTC product pages are functionally sales pages. The 17-Step system below applies whether you're building a dedicated long-scroll page OR optimising the Shopify product page itself.

### When to use both (the full Magic Lantern funnel)

For mid-to-high ticket sales (>$500), use both: ad → opt-in page → HVCO delivered → Magic Lantern email/video sequence → sales page or sales call. The 17-Step system is the sales page. The Magic Lantern is what gets a prospect from "I downloaded a freebie" to "I'm ready to buy."

## Opt-in page structure

Five elements. No more. The opt-in page exists to capture an email — every extra element is friction.

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  HEADLINE       — grabs reader by the throat     │
│  SUBHEADLINE    — restates offer + delivery      │
│                                                  │
│  ┌──────────────┐  • FASCINATION BULLET 1        │
│  │              │  • FASCINATION BULLET 2        │
│  │  VISUAL OF   │  • FASCINATION BULLET 3        │
│  │  THE HVCO    │  • FASCINATION BULLET 4        │
│  │  (mockup)    │  • FASCINATION BULLET 5        │
│  │              │  • FASCINATION BULLET 6        │
│  └──────────────┘                                │
│                                                  │
│  [   NAME    ] [   EMAIL   ]  [  GET INSTANT  ] │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 1. Headline — grab by the throat

Formula: **"Finally! How to get [DESIRED RESULT] without [THING THEY FEAR MOST] in [SPECIFIC TIME FRAME] — Guaranteed."**

Or: **"[PROBLEM]? Get my [SOLUTION] and [RESULT]."**

The headline must:
- Solve ONE specific pain point (not "everything in skincare" — "the bloated belly after every shake")
- Promise a specific, vivid benefit (not "feel better" — "back training next day")
- Use the customer's exact language from `/voc` Section 13 (Language Goldmine)
- Be loud enough to wake a sleepwalking scroller

**Recov example:** *"Finally! Get All 20 Amino Acids in Your Bloodstream in 25 Minutes — Without the Bloat, the Shake, or the Chalky Taste."*

### 2. Subheadline — restate the offer + delivery mechanism

The subheadline tells the reader *what they're getting* and *how they'll receive it*. No mystery.

**Pattern:** *"[Page count / format description] including [specific elements] PLUS [bonus elements]. Simply enter your email below and a PDF copy will instantly be sent to your inbox."*

**Recov example:** *"12-page Recovery Protocol guide covering the 6 mistakes killing your training frequency PLUS our 30-day DOMS-tracking template. Drop your email below and we'll send it straight to your inbox in the next 60 seconds."*

### 3. Fascination bullets — the conversion engine

Bullets do more work than any other element on the opt-in page. They convert curiosity into action. Cap at 6 bullets (more = decision paralysis), each tightly written.

**The 8 fascination bullet formulas (Sabri Suby's set):**

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| **How to X without Y** | "How to get washboard abs without doing a single sit-up" |
| **You need X, right? Wrong!** | "Drinking 3 litres of water a day is healthy, right? WRONG!" |
| **X ways to Y** | "Five ways to meet single women in Melbourne" |
| **Where to find Y** | "Where to find the most ravenous hyperactive buyers online" |
| **How to eliminate X** | "How to stop joint pain forever" |
| **What you should never X** | "What you should never say to a woman on your first date" |
| **Say goodbye to X** | "Say goodbye to calorie counting and hour-long cardio sessions" |
| **The truth about Y** | "The dirty truth about fish oil revealed" |
| **Have you been doing X wrong? (And does it really matter?)** | "Have you been boiling your eggs in the wrong pot? (And does it really matter?)" |
| **The Little-Known Secret to [Y] that [X] Don't Want You to Know** | "The little-known secret to faster recovery that supplement brands don't want you to know" |

**Rule:** every bullet tease, never reveal. The job is to crank curiosity to the breaking point so opting in becomes the only relief.

### 4. Visual representation — show what they're getting

A mockup of the HVCO. If it's a PDF, show a polished 3D-rendered cover. If it's a video, show a play-button thumbnail with the title overlaid. If it's a cheat sheet, show how many pages they're getting (visible stack of pages).

The visual must communicate **perceived value** — the prospect's reaction should be *"that looks like a $50 product they're giving away for free."*

### 5. Form — minimum friction

Two fields only: Name + Email. Every additional field cuts conversion 10-15%. You can collect more details later via the survey/calendar booking page (Phase 6 — see below).

**CTA button copy:** action-specific, benefit-loaded. Not "Submit" or "Sign Up."

Patterns that work:
- "Get Instant Access"
- "Send Me the [HVCO Name]"
- "Yes, Show Me [Benefit]"
- "Download the Guide Now"

## Sales page structure — the 17-Step Secret Selling System

Sabri Suby's 17-step structure has been used to generate $400M+ in sales across 167 niches. It works for:
- Long-scroll landing pages (DTC + info products)
- Sales videos (the script is the same; just spoken)
- Webinar slide decks
- Sales presentations
- Email sales sequences (one step per email)

**Apply ALL 17 steps. Cutting any one degrades the whole.** Most pages that "convert poorly" are missing 4-6 of the steps.

### The full sequence

```
1.  CALL OUT to your audience           ┐
2.  DEMAND attention (big-promise hook)  │  HOOK
3.  BACK UP the promise (subheadline)   ┘

4.  IRRESISTIBLE INTRIGUE (bullets)     ┐  CURIOSITY
5.  FLOODLIGHT on the PROBLEM           │  +
6.  PROVIDE the SOLUTION                ┘  PROBLEM/SOLUTION

7.  SHOW credentials                    ┐  TRUST
8.  DETAIL the BENEFITS                 │  +
9.  SOCIAL PROOF                        ┘  EVIDENCE

10. MAKE Godfather Offer                ┐
11. ADD bonuses                         │
12. STACK the value                     │  OFFER
13. REVEAL the price                    │  (per 14-offer-architecture.md)
14. INJECT scarcity                     │
15. POWER guarantee                     ┘

16. CLEAR call to action
17. P.S. with WARNING + REMINDER
```

### Step-by-step on a real page

**Step 1 — Call out to your audience.** Top of page, before the headline. *"ATTENTION: Australian gym-goers tired of bloated, chalky shakes."* This filters in the right reader and out the wrong one.

**Step 2 — Demand attention with the big-promise headline.** National Enquirer-style. *"How to Get All 20 Amino Acids in Your Bloodstream in 25 Minutes — Without the Bloat, the Shake, or the Chalky Taste."*

**Step 3 — Back up the promise (subheadline).** Straightforward explanation of how. *"Recov is a pre-digested plasma peptide tablet. 6 tablets. Swallow with water. Tested every 2 weeks for banned substances. Used by Olympic weightlifting teams."*

**Step 4 — Irresistible intrigue (bullets, 6 max).** Use the fascination formulas above. Each bullet hits a different pain/fear/hope/dream from `/voc`. *"The little-known reason 68.5% of whey protein users get GI distress (and the simple form-factor swap that eliminates it overnight)."*

**Step 5 — Floodlight the problem.** Identify the audience + agitate the problem so they feel pain. *"You know that 30-minute window after a shake where your stomach feels like a balloon? You've trained yourself to ignore it. But every time you go through it, your gut is signalling that your protein source is wrong for you."* Pull verbatim from `/voc` Section 5 (Pain Points) and Section 13 (Language Goldmine).

**Step 6 — Provide the solution (with mechanism).** Reveal the product and the **unique mechanism** that makes it work where others didn't. *"Recov isn't whey, isn't collagen, isn't plant. It's plasma protein — the same form your body already runs on. Pre-digested into bi-peptides and tri-peptides that absorb in 15-25 minutes instead of 90."* This is where `13-hero-mechanism.md` lands on the page.

**Step 7 — Show credentials.** Trust signals stacked: results achieved, case studies, partner logos, customer counts, press mentions, awards. *"TGA listed. WADA compliant. ASADA compliant. Tested every 2 weeks by the German Olympic Weightlifting team — zero incidents over [X] years."*

**Step 8 — Detail the benefits.** Two-column features → benefits table (from the Detail Sheet exercise in `14-offer-architecture.md`). People care about *benefits*, not features. Talk only in benefits.

**Step 9 — Social proof.** Third-party validation. Verbatim customer testimonials, before/afters, star ratings, review counts, third-party clinical data, expert quotes. Pull straight from `/voc` Section 14 (Social Proof Arsenal).

**Step 10 — Make the Godfather Offer.** This is where `14-offer-architecture.md` lands. The offer must be clear, value-based, concise, persuasive, irresistible. *"Get Recov delivered for $99.95/month — $20 off retail — with a 30-day power guarantee. If you don't see measurably less DOMS in the first 30 days, refund the bottle. Keep the rest of the box."*

**Step 11 — Add bonuses.** Premium stack (see `14-offer-architecture.md` Component 5). *"PLUS: free shipping ($12 value) + 1:1 recovery protocol session with our coach ($150 value) + access to the Recov Members Telegram ($30/month value)."*

**Step 12 — Stack the value.** Visible math. *"$119.95 (Recov) + $12 (shipping) + $150 (coach session) + $30 (community) = $311.95 of value. Today: $99.95/month."*

**Step 13 — Reveal the price.** Frame the price as a relief, not a cost. Compare to what they currently spend ("$4.20 per training session vs. $9.50 for a whey shake that bloats you").

**Step 14 — Inject scarcity.** Real scarcity only. *"Launch cohort capped at 500 bottles. Next production run: 6 weeks."* Countdown clocks work IF they reflect real deadlines.

**Step 15 — Power guarantee.** Specific, measurable, attached to performance. See `14-offer-architecture.md` Component 6. *"If you don't see less DOMS in 30 days, refund the bottle, keep the rest of the box, no questions."*

**Step 16 — Call to action.** ONE command. Specific. Direct. *"Tap the button below to start your first month → "* Not menu of options. Not "learn more." One action.

**Step 17 — P.S. with warning + reminder.** The P.S. is the THIRD most-read element on any sales page (after headline and CTA). Use it.
- Remind them of the Godfather Offer in one sentence
- Warn them of the consequence of NOT buying (cost of inaction)
- Restate the scarcity / urgency
- Repeat the CTA

*"P.S. Every training session you do without Recov is a session your body recovers from in 7 days instead of 2. That's 5 lost training days per session. Over a month of training, that's 20+ sessions you don't get to do. The launch cohort closes when we sell the last of the 500 bottles in this batch. [START YOUR FIRST MONTH →]"*

## The Magic Lantern Technique

For ANY product over ~$300, expecting cold traffic to convert on the first sales page visit is unrealistic. The Larger Market Formula says only **3% of any market is in buying mode right now**. The other 97% need warming up.

The Magic Lantern Technique is the nurture sequence that warms them. It's a 3-4 step value-drip that takes the prospect from "I downloaded a freebie" to "I'm asking you to take my money" — without ever doing a hard sell.

### The structure

```
AD CLICK
   ↓
OPT-IN PAGE              ← 5-element opt-in page (above)
   ↓ (email captured)
HVCO DELIVERY            ← they get what they signed up for
   ↓
THANK-YOU PAGE           ← sales page (17-step system) — captures the 3%
   ↓
EMAIL DRIP (4 STEPS)     ← Magic Lantern — captures more of the 97%
   ├─ Step 1: Audit / checklist / quick win
   ├─ Step 2: Mistakes to avoid / what NOT to do
   ├─ Step 3: Template / specific actionable system
   └─ Step 4: Accelerator / final push
   ↓ (each step ends with soft CTA back to Godfather Offer)
SALES CALL / SALES PAGE  ← prospects arrive here pre-sold, scepticism low
```

### Why it works

Three psychological shifts happen across the sequence:

1. **Scepticism drops.** Every value-only piece they consume without being sold to lowers their defences.
2. **Authority rises.** They start seeing you as a trusted expert, not a vendor.
3. **Desired-outcome proximity increases.** Each step moves them closer to their goal, building belief that the full solution will get them there.

By the time they hit the sales page or sales call at the end of the sequence, they're warm. The close stops being a battle and becomes a transaction.

### The 4-step Magic Lantern formula

For each step, pick one tactical "milestone" the prospect needs to hit on their way to the desired outcome. Each step delivers one milestone.

**Each piece follows the 80/20 rule:** 80% value, 20% soft pitch back to the Godfather Offer at the end.

#### Step 1 — The Audit / Checklist

Give them a way to evaluate where they are right now. A 12-step audit, a 10-point checklist, a quick-fire diagnostic. The job: make them more self-aware about the problem.

**Recov example:** *"The 8-Point Recovery Audit — find out exactly which bottleneck is killing your training frequency."*

#### Step 2 — The Mistakes / What Not To Do

Mistakes content outperforms benefits content. People are wired to avoid loss more than seek gain. Show them the 5-10 most common mistakes in their space and frame the next email as the "what TO do" follow-up.

**Recov example:** *"The 11 Recovery Mistakes Sabotaging Your Gains — and what to do instead."*

#### Step 3 — The Template / Specific System

Hand them a usable template, framework, or playbook. Their reaction should be *"this alone would have been worth paying for."* This is where the relationship shifts from "interested" to "compelled."

**Recov example:** *"The 30-Day DOMS Reduction Protocol — week-by-week training and recovery calendar, free to download and use."*

#### Step 4 — The Accelerator

The final piece. Show them how to compound the results from steps 1-3. This step is where the soft pitch becomes a clear invitation: *"if you want to skip the learning curve, here's the offer."*

**Recov example:** *"How to compress your recovery window by another 40% using the same protocol Olympic weightlifters use. [Book a recovery protocol call →]"*

### Each step's structure

Every email + video pair follows this structure:

```
EMAIL:
1. Open loop in the subject line
2. Tease the value (don't reveal it)
3. Link to the video / asset

VIDEO (3-7 minutes):
1. Hook (0-15s) — call back to the open loop, promise the payoff
2. Deliver the value (80% of runtime) — actually give the result
3. Soft CTA (last 20%) — "If you liked this and you're committed to [outcome], I've put aside some time to help you solve this directly. Here's the Godfather Offer..."

EMAIL FOLLOW-UP (1-2 days later):
1. Reference the value they just consumed
2. Tease the next piece (open the next loop)
3. Link to the next video
```

Drip rhythm: each step lands 1-2 days after the previous. Don't bunch them — let each piece marinate. The hint at the end of each email *"You thought this was great? Wait till you see what's coming Wednesday..."* keeps open rates above 50% across the sequence.

## How to brief `automaitions` for landing page production

The `automaitions-html-builder` skill (the other half of this repo) is the actual production tool. It builds polished, animated HTML landing pages from a brief.

When briefing `automaitions` for a landing page that's part of the AI Ad Lab chain, supply:

### Required inputs

1. **The Brand DNA document** (from `/brand-dna`). `automaitions` will use the palette, typography, photo direction, and 72-word prompt modifier directly.
2. **The Godfather Offer paragraph** (from `14-offer-architecture.md`). 4-6 lines covering all 7 components.
3. **Page type:** opt-in OR sales OR Magic Lantern step. Different structures.
4. **The 17-step copy** (if it's a sales page) — written out per the system above. `automaitions` produces the *visual treatment*; the copy must be written before the build.
5. **The hero mechanism** (from `13-hero-mechanism.md`) — for the Step 6 "Provide the solution" section.
6. **Customer quotes** (from `/voc` Section 14 Social Proof Arsenal) — for the Step 9 Social Proof section.

### Briefing template

```
SKILL: automaitions-html-builder
PAGE TYPE: [opt-in / sales / magic-lantern-step-N]
BRAND DNA: [paste / attach brand-dna.html]
GODFATHER OFFER: [4-6 line paragraph from 14-offer-architecture.md]
HERO MECHANISM: [1-sentence mechanism statement from 13-hero-mechanism.md]
COPY: [full 17-step copy if sales page, or 5-element copy if opt-in]
ASSETS: [product images, HVCO mockup, headshot, etc.]
DEPLOY TARGET: [Vercel / Netlify / Shopify section / standalone HTML]
```

### What automaitions handles

- Visual treatment (palette, typography, motion, responsive layout)
- Mobile-first responsive design with desktop polish
- Logo embedding, favicon, OG meta tags
- Mandatory hamburger nav (per `automaitions/skill/SKILL.md`)
- Animation tier selection (proposes Tier 3 motion candidate, awaits confirmation)
- The audit pass before delivery

### What you brief separately

- The copy itself (this file's job)
- The strategic decisions (what offer, what mechanism, which customer type)
- The conversion architecture (opt-in vs sales vs nurture step)

`automaitions` is a *production* skill, not a *strategy* skill. Same pattern as `/create-static-ads` — strategy comes from CA, production comes from automaitions.

## Common mistakes to avoid

| Mistake | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Skipping the 17 steps (using 8-10) | Each step handles a specific objection or psychological move. Skipping any leaves an unhandled objection in the page. | Include all 17. Edit ruthlessly for length, but keep every step represented. |
| Putting the offer in the hero | Cold traffic isn't ready. The offer needs proof + mechanism + social proof first. | Hero = headline + promise + intrigue bullets. Offer lives in steps 10-15 (lower half of page). |
| Sales page for a $5K product without Magic Lantern | Cold traffic to high-ticket sales page = 0.1% conversion. | High-ticket needs opt-in + nurture + sales call. Cold traffic to sales page only works under $300. |
| Magic Lantern with "buy now" emails | Trains the audience to ignore your emails. | Each email = 80% value, 20% soft CTA. Hard sell only in the final accelerator step. |
| Fake scarcity / fabricated MSRP | Conversions short-term, reputation long-term. | Real scarcity only. Real anchor prices only. See `14-offer-architecture.md` ethical guardrails. |
| One CTA at the top + 5 different CTAs throughout | Decision paralysis. | ONE CTA. Repeated. Same wording. Same destination. |
| Generic stock photos | "This looks like every other sales page" — buyer disengages | Brand-specific photography per `/brand-dna` photo direction. Or AI-generated via `/seedance-*` for hero imagery. |
| Page that doesn't match the ad | Ad/page mismatch breaks the promise → bounce rate explodes | Headline + hero image on landing page must echo the ad creative directly. |

## Landing page inspiration — where to look at what's converting RIGHT NOW

The 17-Step Selling System above is the timeless structural framework. But specific visual treatments, section orderings, social-proof formats, modal patterns, sticky-CTA implementations, and category-specific conventions shift constantly. Before building any new landing page, scan what's actually working in the niche right now.

### Public swipefile sites — use these via WebFetch

| Site | Best for | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **pages.xyz** | DTC landing pages with ad attribution — pages that real brands are spending on | Cold-traffic DTC pages, ecom, supplements |
| **swiped.co** | Classic direct response sales letters — Halbert, Schwartz, Carlton-era + modern DR | Long-form sales letters, info products, coaching |
| **land-book.com** | Design-led landing page gallery | Premium / minimal / aesthetic brands |
| **lapa.ninja** | Landing page library searchable by category | Quick category-specific reference |
| **onepagelove.com** | Single-page sites by industry | Personal brand, founder, portfolio |
| **saaslandingpage.com** | SaaS-specific patterns | B2B SaaS, agency, consultancy |
| **web.archive.org** | Historical versions of any URL | Studying how high-performers evolved their LP over 6-24 months |

### Workflow — when starting a new landing page

```
1. Identify 3-5 competitor URLs in the niche (use /spy or just user knowledge)
2. WebFetch each one — pull the HTML and extract structure
3. Note: which 17-Step elements are present? Which are missing? In what order?
4. Identify the "Sea of Sameness" — what 80% of competitors do
5. Identify the gap — what NONE of them are doing well
6. Brief CA's strategic brief to LEAN INTO that gap when constructing your page
7. Brief automaitions per `17-automaitions-handoff.md` with the gap as a differentiation note
```

### Paid tools — when you need depth

- **Particl** (~$300/mo) — DTC competitive intelligence. Tracks landing page CHANGES over time on every major DTC brand. Best-in-class for "what just shifted in our category."
- **MagicBrief / Foreplay** — DTC-focused, integrates ad + LP analysis.
- **SimilarWeb** — traffic + funnel analysis.

These are situational — only invest in a paid tool if you're running 10+ DTC brands or scaling past $100K/mo Meta spend per brand.

### Future skill — `/lp-spy`

`/lp-spy` is a planned 25th skill in the AI Ad Lab suite — the landing-page equivalent of `/spy` for ads. Takes a competitor URL or niche keyword, scrapes the page(s), takes screenshots, extracts structural patterns, scores against the 17-Step Selling System, outputs a scored HTML swipe file. Will feed visual reference into `automaitions` alongside CA's strategic brief.

Until `/lp-spy` exists, use the WebFetch workflow above against the swipefile sites listed.

### Visual attention validation — Attention AI

**Before driving paid traffic to any landing page (yours or any competitor's), validate the attention flow.** See `19-attention-ai.md` for the full playbook.

For competitor pages: run them through Attention AI to see where eyes actually land — not just what the structure looks like. A page can have all 17 steps but if the heatmap is shotgun, conversion will be weak.

For your own pages after `automaitions` ships them: see Workflow A in `19-attention-ai.md` — 5-minute pre-launch QA that closes the biggest gap in current landing page production (no objective visual QA).
